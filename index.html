Contrast

This image uses the design principle of contrast. Contrast is when 2 things are strikingly different, often juxtaposing somethign else directly related. This is often used in Media to make content look distuingishable, highlighting elements in a piece of media, or adding a unique flair to the media.

Alignment

This image is an example of Alignment. Alignment is when text and elements of media are aligned according to an axis. My leaflet does this simply by aligning everything onto the vertical centre axis, from the logo to all the key details.

Repetition

This is an example of repetition. Repetition, as it's name would suggest is the principle of using repetition to convey a message, establish a style, or to just make people remember your name or slogan, as I have done.

Proximity

This is an example of proximity. Proximity makes great use of forcing every element of a piece of media close together, but in a neat and organised fashion. This makes the media piece look busier, but also follow a logical and precise order that is easy to follow.

Axial Typography

Axial Typography, as the name suggests works on both horizontal and vertical axis. It is based on straight lines and such, creating a neat but sometimes chaotic web of lines on which the text sits.

Bilateral Typography

Bilateral typography is similar to axial in that they both tend to pivot around a vertical or horizontal axis. Bilateral is very organised as sit is usually parallel to anything it might contrast with.

Dilatational Typography

Dilatational Typography is based on the typography extending from an inner point, or wrapping around an existing circular object. These typefaces have little useful application as the text becomes quite unreadable due to the warping, however they prodivde a unique visual flair.

Grid Typography

Grid typographies, are one of the more useful types of typography. They are based on grids, squares that are split up on a page, filled with content. It is good for modern day aesthetics, as well as clean cohesive layouts.

Modular Typography

Modular typography is similar to grid typography, but even more extreme. Modular typography works stricly in squares. It can be adapted to any format, as the lenghts of the squares can be anything, which makes it good for laying out text. It is one of the more popular and used typefaces.

Radial Typeography

Radial typography works in circles. Somewhat similar to dilatational, it serves to utilise the shape of a circle. However for Radial typography, the text tends to rest on beams extruding from the circle. This provides a unique visual typeface that is also still very readable.

Random Typography

Random typography, is as the name suggests, very random. It does not follow any set rules or have any distinct styles. This makes it very free and full of potential to create something truly visually unique.

Transitional Typography

Radial typogrpahy works like the ocean, in waves. This is one of the more confusing and less useful typefaces, as the lack of rules and regulations of it, as well as coherence makes it quite useless when trying to deliver a message.
